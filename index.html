Poppins

Font designed by Johnny Pinhorn and Ninad Kale with the Indian Type Foundry

Poppins follows the tradition of geometric sans serif typefaces that have been popular in typography since the 1920s. Poppins typeface is an eyecatching classically designed sans-serif font. First released in 2014, it was designed by Jonny Pinhorn and Ninad Kale with the Indian Type Foundry. Poppins is an open source typeface that not only supports Latin characters but also Devanagari script characters used in Indian languages.

Poppins font is credited for being the first large Devanagari family in this style, making Poppins a truly global typeface. Poppins typeface includes 9 weights from thin through black, and each weight includes an italics version. Making the font versatile enough to be used as headlines or body text fitting into any design modern or traditional.  

Poppins is a bold, simple, modern font featuring elegant geometric shapes, such as circles, as the basis of its design. The Poppins font features a very high x-height, large counters, large bowls, and sharp angular curves. The lowercase “p” has a thicker round edge, the cutout of the “p” aligns with the stem instead of being centred, which creates its distinct appearance. The “p” looks like a perfect circle, but a closer look shows it is not the same thickness. Every letter is nearly monolinear, with optical corrections applied to stroke joints to keep an even typographic appearance.  

Poppins is a free web font available for use through Google and Adobe Fonts. Poppins is a good alternative for commercial fonts Futura and Avant Garde. You’ll find Poppins being used by large and small businesses of all types.
